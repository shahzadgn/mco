Core Objectives

1

Data Integrity & Value-Based Measurement

Shift the measurement framework from volume-based "vanity metrics" (e.g., PDF downloads) to value-based outcomes. We will restrict Primary Conversions in ad platforms to high-intent actions—specifically Demo Requests and Contact Sales—ensuring smart bidding algorithms optimize purely for revenue-generating events.

2

Precision Targeting & ABM Alignment

Eliminate budget wastage on unqualified audiences by refining LinkedIn targeting logic. We will transition from "Job Title OR Account" to a strict "Job Title AND Account List" structure, guaranteeing that impressions are delivered exclusively to key decision-makers within our defined Tier-1 ABM accounts.

3

Budget Efficiency & Pillar Focus

Maximize the impact of the $14k monthly budget by concentrating capital on high-performance areas. We will allocate 80% of spend to the "Compliance Management" and "Regulatory Change" pillars where intent is highest, while implementing strict caps on broad, experimental, or low-converting campaigns.

Google Ads Roadmap

Paid Search Execution Plan

Month 1: The Fix (Stabilization)

Weeks 1-4
  • Conversion Audit & Cleanup This is the critical first step. We will audit GA4 and Google Ads conversion events to differentiate between "soft" conversions (PDF downloads) and "hard" conversions (Demo Requests). By marking only "Book Demo" as Primary, we ensure the smart bidding algorithms optimize for revenue-generating actions rather than just volume.
  • URL Hygiene A full crawl of active ad destination URLs will be conducted. Any 404 errors (broken links) waste budget immediately. We will implement 301 redirects for any legacy product pages to ensure every click lands on a live, relevant page.
  • Spend Stabilization Erratic spending confuses Google's learning phase. We will implement consistent daily budget caps to smooth out spending, preventing "feast or famine" days that spike CPA without delivering results.

Month 2: The Refine (Optimization)

Weeks 5-8
  • Match Type Constriction We will transition core keywords (like "Regulatory Change Management") from Broad Match to Phrase and Exact Match. Broad match often pulls in irrelevant consumer queries; this shift ensures we only pay for B2B intent, improving our Cost Per Acquisition (CPA).
  • Search Term Mining Weekly analysis of search terms to identify wasted spend. We will aggressively add negative keywords for consumer-intent terms (e.g., "jobs," "free," "course," "definition") to refine the traffic quality further.
  • RSA Rebuild Responsive Search Ads (RSAs) will be rewritten to focus on specific pain points and solutions rather than generic features. Improving Ad Strength to "Good" or "Excellent" typically lowers CPCs by increasing Quality Score.

Month 3: The Scale (Growth)

Weeks 9-12
  • Paid Landing Pages We will launch dedicated landing pages for the top 20% of high-volume keywords. These pages will have no navigation bar (to prevent leakiness) and will focus entirely on the value proposition and the "Book Demo" form, significantly boosting conversion rates.
  • Smart Bidding Activation With 60 days of clean conversion data now available, we will switch campaigns to Target CPA (tCPA) bidding. This allows Google's AI to bid more aggressively for users most likely to convert, maximizing the return on our ad spend.

LinkedIn Ads Roadmap

Paid Social & ABM Strategy

Phase 1: Foundation (The "AND" Fix)

The primary issue identified is loose targeting logic. Currently, campaigns may be using "Job Title OR Account List," which allows irrelevant employees (e.g., interns at a target bank) to see ads. We will switch all campaigns to "Job Title AND Account List" logic. This ensures every impression serves a specific decision-maker at a specific target account, drastically improving spend efficiency.

Funnel Reset:

We will restructure campaigns into distinct TOF (Awareness), MOF (Case Studies), and BOF (Demo Request) layers to ensure appropriate messaging for each stage of the buyer journey.

Creative Baseline:

Duplicate creatives will be paused. We will select one "control" creative per concept to establish a reliable performance baseline for future A/B testing.

Phase 2: Expansion

Once the core Investment Banking audience is stabilized, we will expand our reach. We will launch dedicated campaigns targeting the Asset Management, Insurance, and FinTech sectors to identify new Total Addressable Market (TAM). Simultaneously, we will begin A/B testing messaging hooks: "Risk Reduction" (fear-based) vs. "Audit Readiness" (benefit-based).

Phase 3: ROI Alignment

With a refined audience and validated messaging, we will shift the majority of the budget towards Bottom-of-Funnel (BOF) campaigns to drive Demo requests. Crucially, we will integrate LinkedIn campaign data with HubSpot to track "Pipeline Influenced"—measuring success by deal value created, not just lead volume.
